  • Katherine Johnson

    A mathematician whose calculations of orbital mechanics as a NASA
    employee were critical to the success of the firstand subsequent
    U.S. manned spaceflights during her 35-year career at NASA .

  • Grace Hopper

    A pioneer of computer programming who invented one of the first compiler
    related tools. She popularized the idea of machine-independent programming
    languages, which led to the development of COBOL, an early high-level
    programming language still in use today.

  • Annie Easley

    A computer scientist, mathematician, and rocket scientist. She
    worked for the Lewis Research Center (now Glenn Research Center)
    of the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA) and its
    predecessor, the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA)

  • Ada Lovelace

    An English mathematician known for her work on Charles Babbage's
    proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.
    She published the first algorithm intended to be carried out by such a machine.

  • Anita Borg

    An American computer scientist. She founded the Institutefor Women and
    Technology and the Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing.

  • Mary Kenneth Keller

    an American Roman Catholic religious sister, educator
    and pioneer in computer science. She was the first woman
    to earn a Ph.D. in computer science in the United States.

The Association for Computing Machinery (ACM-W) CUNY Celebrates Women in Computing invites participation in its inaugural conference on Friday, May 10, 2019, at the Microsoft Midtown Manhattan Office, New York City.

The CUNYCWiC aims to encourage the engagement and the participation of women and men in a wide range of computer science related research and projects and to provide networking opportunities and explorations of careers in computing.
